# Italian scholarly sources for "La doppia kenosi del linguaggio teologico"

This bibliography assembles **over 100 Italian-language scholarly sources** — monographs, edited volumes, critical editions, translations, and journal articles — organized across the ten thematic sections of the article. Each entry supports the article's core argument that theological language undergoes a "double kenosis": emptying itself of ordinary semantic content to gesture toward the divine, while simultaneously receiving new meaning through the Christological-incarnational dynamic. Sources are drawn from the major Italian academic publishers (Vita e Pensiero, Bompiani, Adelphi, Morcelliana, Città Nuova, Carocci, Jaca Book, Le Lettere, Olschki, Mimesis) and leading Italian journals. Priority has been given to works from **2000–2025**, with foundational earlier works included where indispensable.

## 1. Pseudo-Dionigi Areopagita e teologia apofatica

This section treats the Corpus Dionysiacum as the paradigmatic matrix of Christian apophatic theology — the triadic movement of cataphasis, apophasis, and hyperoche, the distinction between negation and privation, and the image of the "tenebra sopralucente."

**Critical editions and translations**

- Dionigi Areopagita, *Tutte le opere*, testo greco a fronte, a cura di Piero Scazzoso ed Enzo Bellini, Milano, Bompiani ("Il pensiero occidentale"), 2009. — The standard complete Italian translation of the entire Corpus Dionysiacum, including *De mystica theologia*, *De divinis nominibus*, *De coelesti hierarchia*, *De ecclesiastica hierarchia*, and the *Lettere*. Originally published by Rusconi; the Bompiani reissue includes the critical Greek text with scholarly apparatus.

- Edith Stein, *Vie della conoscenza di Dio. Saggio su Dionigi l'Areopagita*, a cura di Angela Ales Bello e Marco Paolinelli, Roma, Città Nuova / Edizioni OCD, 2020, pp. 128. — Italian translation of Stein's 1941 study exploring the Corpus Dionysiacum from a phenomenological standpoint. Examines graded forms of divine knowledge — cataphatic, apophatic, and mystical — and the epistemological limits of language before God.

**Monographs**

- Salvatore Lilla, *Dionigi l'Areopagita e il platonismo cristiano*, Brescia, Morcelliana ("Letteratura cristiana antica"), 2005. — Major scholarly monograph by the Vatican Library specialist, analyzing the synthesis of Platonic/Neoplatonic philosophy and Christian theology in Pseudo-Dionysius. Treats negative theology from classical Greek thought through the Cappadocians to the Dionysian system of hierarchies, symbolism, and apophaticism.

- Ernesto Sergio Mainoldi, *Dietro «Dionigi l'Areopagita». La genesi e gli scopi del Corpus Dionysiacum*, Roma, Città Nuova ("Paradigma medievale – Institutiones"), 2018, pp. 632. — Innovative monograph proposing a new authorship hypothesis (a student of Proclus/Damascius who converted to Christianity). Treats the synthesis of Neoplatonic metaphysics with patristic tradition, the question of negative theology, deification, and hierarchical ontology.

- Diego Sbacchi, *La presenza di Dionigi Areopagita nel Paradiso di Dante*, Firenze, Leo S. Olschki, 2006. — On the reception of Dionysian theology (including the apophatic structure and celestial hierarchy) in Dante's *Paradiso*. Important for understanding how Dionysian apophaticism entered the mainstream of Italian intellectual culture.

**Articles and essays**

- Nicolò Sassi, "Le fonti del lessico teologico del *De Mystica Theologia* dello Pseudo-Dionigi Areopagita," in *Textual Cultures*, vol. 11, no. 1/2, 2017, pp. 130–171. — Italian-language article analyzing the theological vocabulary and literary/philosophical/liturgical sources of the *De mystica theologia* through lexical and syntagmatic analysis.

- Michele Abbate, *Tra esegesi e teologia. Studi sul neoplatonismo*, Milano-Udine, Mimesis ("Askesis"), 2012. — Collected studies on Neoplatonic philosophy including analysis of Pseudo-Dionysius, the solar metaphor of the First Principle, and hermeneutics in late-antique Neoplatonism. Directly relevant to the Neoplatonic matrix underlying Dionysian apophaticism.

- René Roques, *L'universo dionisiano. Struttura gerarchica del mondo secondo ps. Dionigi Areopagita*, Milano, Vita e Pensiero, 1996 (traduzione italiana). — Italian translation of the classic French study on the hierarchical architecture of Dionysian thought.

## 2. Anselmo d'Aosta e la semantica del doppio registro

The article reads Anselm's *De grammatico* distinction between *significatio per se* and *significatio per aliud* as the key to understanding how theological predicates function in the *Monologion* and *Proslogion* — especially the "Deus est iustitia" thesis and the treatment of misericordia in *Proslogion* VIII.

**Critical editions and translations**

- Sofia Vanni Rovighi (a cura di), Anselmo d'Aosta, *Opere filosofiche*, Laterza, Roma-Bari, 1969; nuova edizione rivista da Pietro B. Rossi, 2008, pp. 344. — The most authoritative Italian translation of Anselm's philosophical works: *Monologion*, *Proslogion*, *De grammatico*, *De veritate*, *De libertate arbitrii*, *De casu diaboli*, *De concordia*. Vanni Rovighi's introduction and notes attend carefully to semantic and logical issues.

- Italo Sciuto (a cura di), Anselmo d'Aosta, *Monologio e Proslogio. Testo latino a fronte*, Bompiani, Milano, 2002, pp. 526. — Critical edition with facing Latin text, extensive introduction, and updated bibliography. Sciuto's commentary addresses the semantic structure of the Proslogion arguments, including divine attributes and misericordia in ch. VIII.

**Monographs and introductions**

- Sofia Vanni Rovighi, *Introduzione a Anselmo d'Aosta*, Laterza, Roma-Bari, 1987 (rist. 1999), pp. 184. — Classic introductory monograph by Italy's foremost Anselmian interpreter. Covers the *De grammatico*, the *significatio/appellatio* distinction, semantic theory applied to theology, and the arguments of the *Monologion* and *Proslogion*. Foundational for the Italian reception of Anselm's double-register semantics.

- Giuseppe Colombo, *Invito al pensiero di Sant'Anselmo*, Mursia, Milano, 1990 (rist. 2022), pp. 152. — Scholarly introduction covering all major works, including the relationship between logical analysis, theology, and linguistic semantics, relevant to the "Deus est iustitia" thesis and divine simplicity.

- Massimo Parodi, *Il conflitto dei pensieri. Studio su Anselmo d'Aosta*, Lubrina, Bergamo, 1988. — Includes the essay "Improprietas verborum" on the improper use of language in Anselm, treating the *De grammatico*'s distinctions and the relationship of linguistic analysis to theology.

**Articles**

- Luigi Catalani, "Il postulato dell'ordine in Anselmo d'Aosta," in *Quaestio*, 2004, pp. 7–33. — Examines Anselm's presupposition of ontological order, directly relevant to his theological semantics and the framework within which divine predication operates.

- Silvio Tafuri, "Il problema della significazione nel pensiero di Anselmo," in *Anselmo d'Aosta e il pensiero monastico medievale* (Atti del XVIII Congresso della SISPM), Brepols, 2013. — Specifically on the problem of signification (*significazione*) in Anselm's thought, from the Italian Society for the Study of Medieval Thought.

- "«Nec plus nec minus». Logica ed etica del linguaggio nel «De veritate» di Anselmo d'Aosta," in A. Bisogno, L. Catalani, A. Cavallini, R. de Filippis (a cura di), *«Saepe mihi cogitanti…» Studi di filosofia tardo-antica, medievale e umanistica offerti a Giulio d'Onofrio*, 2023. — Very recent Italian scholarship (2023) directly on logic and ethics of language in Anselm's *De veritate*.

- Roberto Limonta, "Linguaggio e spazio del silenzio in Anselmo d'Aosta" (article, c. 2010s). — Addresses the theme of language (*significatio*) in Anselm, drawing on *De veritate* and the distinction between *significatio* and *rectitudo*, and the limits of words before divine truth.

## 3. Alano di Lilla e il Liber viginti quattuor philosophorum

The article deploys the geometric metaphor of the *sphaera infinita/intelligibilis* from the *Liber XXIV philosophorum* and Alanus's *Regulae caelestis iuris* as a paradigmatic instance of how medieval theologians used formal structures to express the inexpressible.

**Critical editions and translations**

- Paolo Lucentini (a cura di), *Il libro dei ventiquattro filosofi*, Milano, Adelphi (Piccola Biblioteca Adelphi, 429), 1999, pp. 150. — **The** reference Italian edition. Contains three foundational essays by Lucentini: "Origine e natura del *Libro dei ventiquattro filosofi*," the critical Latin text with Italian translation, and "La fortuna del *Libro dei ventiquattro filosofi* nel Medioevo." Essential for the *sphaera infinita* metaphor and the reception by Alanus, Eckhart, and Cusanus.

- Piercarlo Necchi (a cura di), *Il libro dei XXIV filosofi*, Genova, Il Melangolo, 1996. — Earlier Italian edition with introduction, translation, and notes. A useful complement to the Lucentini edition.

- Alano di Lilla, *Sulle tracce di Dio. Regulae caelestis iuris. Sermo de sphaera intelligibili*, prima traduzione italiana con testo latino a fronte, introduzione e note a cura di Marco Rossini, Brescia, Morcelliana, 2001. — **First Italian translation** of both the *Regulae caelestis iuris* and the *Sermo de sphaera intelligibili*. Directly relevant to the geometric/sphere metaphors in Alanus's theology.

**Monographs and collected volumes**

- Pina Totaro – Luisa Valente (a cura di), *Sphaera. Forma, immagine e metafora tra Medioevo ed età moderna*, Firenze, Olschki ("Lessico intellettuale europeo," 117), 2012. — Major collected volume directly on the sphere metaphor, containing three chapters of central relevance:
  - **Paolo Lucentini**, "La sfera infinita e la fortuna della seconda proposizione del Libro dei XXIV filosofi nel medioevo," pp. 1–12 (Lucentini's final treatment of the topic).
  - **Luisa Valente**, "Sfera infinita e sfera intellegibile: immaginazione e conoscenza di Dio nel Libro dei XXIV filosofi e in Alano di Lilla."
  - **Alessandra Beccarisi**, "«Deus est sphaera intellectualis infinita»: Eckhart interprete del *Liber XXIV philosophorum*," pp. 167–192 (analyzes Eckhart's reception of Proposition II).

- Paolo Lucentini, *Platonismo, ermetismo, eresia nel Medioevo*, Louvain-La-Neuve, FIDEM, 2007. — Collected essays on Platonic and Hermetic traditions, providing broader context for the *Liber XXIV* within the Hermetic Latin tradition.

- Paolo Lucentini – Vittoria Perrone Compagni, *I testi e i codici di Ermete nel Medioevo*, Firenze, Edizioni Polistampa, 2001. — Reference work on the manuscript tradition of Hermetic texts, directly relevant to the transmission of the *Liber XXIV philosophorum*.

- Luisa Valente, "Filosofia e teologia nelle opere di Alano di Lilla: pluralità delle forme letterarie e unitarietà del sapere." — Study of the unity of Alanus's diverse literary and theological-philosophical output, contextualizing the *Regulae* within his broader corpus.

- Ilaria Parri, "Note sul Libro dei ventiquattro filosofi," in S. Caroti – R. Pinzani (a cura di), *Ob rogatum meorum sociorum. Studi in memoria di Lorenzo Pozzi*, Franco Angeli, Milano 2000, pp. 155–170. — Scholarly notes on the *Liber XXIV*, addressing philosophical and textual issues.

## 4. Meister Eckhart: esse, negatio negationis, Grund

The article treats Eckhart's paradoxical oscillation between *esse est Deus* and *esse formaliter non est in Deo*, his recasting of Dionysian *negatio negationis* as *purissima affirmatio*, the *Grund/Gottheit* doctrine, and the 1329 condemnation *In agro dominico*.

**Critical editions and translations (Marco Vannini)**

- Meister Eckhart, *Sermoni tedeschi*, a cura di Marco Vannini, Milano, Adelphi, 1985 (7ª ed.), pp. 278. — Classic edition of 36 German sermons; foundational text for Italian Eckhart reception.

- Meister Eckhart, *Dell'uomo nobile*, a cura di Marco Vannini, Milano, Adelphi, 1999, pp. 242. — Contains all Treatises: *Istruzioni spirituali*, *Del distacco*, *Il libro della consolazione divina*, *Dell'uomo nobile*.

- Meister Eckhart, *I sermoni*, a cura di Marco Vannini, Milano, Edizioni Paoline, 2002. — Complete translation of all 104 German sermons from the critical edition (Quint/Steer). The most comprehensive Italian volume of Eckhart's German preaching.

- Meister Eckhart, *Commenti all'Antico Testamento*, a cura di Marco Vannini, Milano, Bompiani ("Il pensiero occidentale"), 2012, pp. 1548. — Monumental omnibus of the Latin exegetical works: commentaries on Genesis, Exodus, Wisdom, Ecclesiasticus. Essential for the *esse/Deus* paradox in Eckhart's Latin thought.

- Meister Eckhart, *Commento al vangelo di Giovanni*, a cura di Marco Vannini, Milano, Bompiani, 2017. — Key Latin work containing major discussions on *esse formaliter non est in Deo* and the Johannine Prologue.

- Meister Eckhart, *Meister Eckhart e il fondo dell'anima*, a cura di Marco Vannini, Roma, Città Nuova, 1991. — Contains the **Quaestiones Parisienses I and II** — the critical Paris disputations where Eckhart argues that *intelligere* is higher than *esse*.

- Meister Eckhart, *L'anima e Dio sono una cosa sola*, a cura di Marco Vannini, Firenze, Le Lettere, 2020. — 25 sermons (Latin and German), some previously unpublished in Italian.

**Critical editions (Loris Sturlese)**

- Meister Eckhart, *Le 64 prediche sul tempo liturgico*, introduzione, traduzione, note e apparati di Loris Sturlese, Milano, Bompiani ("Il pensiero occidentale"), 2014, pp. LXXXVII + 925. — Landmark scholarly edition: first-ever reconstruction of the liturgical sermon cycle, with Middle High German facing text, rigorous Italian translation, and an 87-page interpretive introduction arguing for Eckhart as philosopher rather than mystic.

**Italian scholarly monographs**

- Loris Sturlese, *Eckhart, Tauler, Suso. Filosofi e mistici nella Germania medievale*, Firenze, Le Lettere ("Quaderni del Giornale Critico della Filosofia Italiana," 17), 2010, pp. 272. — Major study arguing that Eckhart's project is fundamentally philosophical rather than mystical. Examines the "mystical triad" of late medieval Germany.

- Marco Vannini, *Introduzione a Eckhart. Profilo e testi*, Firenze, Le Lettere, 2014. — Scholarly introduction culminating Vannini's 40-year engagement with Eckhart.

- Marco Vannini, *Prego Dio che mi liberi da Dio. La religione come verità e come menzogna*, Milano, Bompiani, 2010. — Engages Eckhart's most radical statements to develop a theology of the *Gottheit* beyond God.

- Marco Vannini, *Beati pauperes spiritu. Attualità di Meister Eckhart*, Torino, Lindau, 2022. — Most recent Vannini monograph on Eckhart's enduring relevance.

- Alain De Libera, *Meister Eckhart e la mistica renana*, trad. it. A. Granata, Milano, Jaca Book ("Eredità medievale"), 1998, pp. 128. — Italian translation of De Libera's focused study on Eckhart within the Rhenish mystical movement.

- Alain De Libera, *Introduzione alla mistica renana. Da Alberto Magno a Meister Eckhart*, trad. it., Milano, Jaca Book, 1999, pp. 374. — Comprehensive treatment of the Cologne school from Albert the Great through Eckhart.

- M.A. Vannier – M. Vannini (a cura di), *I mistici renani. Eckhart, Taulero, Suso*, Milano, Jaca Book, 2013. — Anthology of Rhenish mystics with scholarly apparatus.

**Articles**

- Loris Sturlese, "Mistica o filosofia? A proposito della dottrina dell'immagine di Meister Eckhart," in *Giornale Critico della Filosofia Italiana*, 12 (1), 1992, pp. 49–64. — Key article on the *Bildelehre* (doctrine of the image).

- Alessandra Beccarisi, "Libertà e intelletto: Una lettura di Eckhart, Predica I," in *Giornale Critico della Filosofia Italiana*, 23 (3), 2003. — Close philosophical reading of Eckhart's Sermon I on freedom and intellect.

- Alessandra Beccarisi – Alessandro Palazzo (a cura di), *Per studium et doctrinam. Fonti e testi di filosofia medievale dal XII al XIV secolo. Studi in onore di Loris Sturlese*, Roma, Aracne ("Flumen Sapientiae," 6), 2018, pp. 254. — Festschrift with multiple chapters on medieval philosophy including Eckhart's milieu.

## 5. Tommaso d'Aquino: nomi divini e analogia

The article appeals to *Summa Theologiae* I, q. 13 for its distinction between *res significata* and *modus significandi*, the doctrine of *ipsum esse subsistens*, the three ways (*via causalitatis, remotionis, eminentiae*), and the defence of analogical predication against both Maimonidean equivocity and Scotist univocity.

**Monographs**

- Pasquale Porro, *Tommaso d'Aquino. Un profilo storico-filosofico*, Roma, Carocci, 2012 (Quality Paperbacks, 2018), pp. 535. — The most comprehensive and authoritative Italian monograph on Aquinas. Treats the entire Thomistic corpus chronologically, including the doctrine of divine names (q. 13), divine simplicity (q. 3), and the analogical theory of predication. Porro is the leading Italian Aquinas scholar.

- Giovanni Ventimiglia, *To be o esse? La questione dell'essere nel tomismo analitico*, Roma, Carocci, 2012. — On *ipsum esse subsistens*, the analogy of being, and the predication of *esse* in Aquinas, in dialogue with Geach, Kenny, and the analytic tradition.

- Giovanni Ventimiglia, *Differenza e contraddizione. Il problema dell'essere in Tommaso d'Aquino: esse, diversum, contradictio*, Milano, Vita e Pensiero, 1997. — In-depth study of the problem of being in Aquinas, with attention to the real distinction and *ipsum esse*.

- Battista Mondin, *La metafisica di S. Tommaso d'Aquino e i suoi interpreti*, Bologna, Edizioni Studio Domenicano, 2002. — Comprehensive survey of Thomistic metaphysics including explicit treatment of divine names, analogy (proportionality, attribution), transcendentals, and *res significata / modus significandi*.

- Battista Mondin, *Dizionario enciclopedico del pensiero di san Tommaso d'Aquino*, Bologna, Edizioni Studio Domenicano, 1991 (2ª ed. aggiornata 2000). — Encyclopedic entries on *analogia*, *nomi divini*, *esse*, *linguaggio teologico*, *univocità/equivocità*.

- Franco Riva, *Analogia e univocità in Tommaso De Vio "Gaetano"*, Milano, Vita e Pensiero, 1995. — Study of the transformation of the Thomistic doctrine of analogy in Cajetan, with extensive treatment of being, the good, and divine names as analogical predicates.

- Anthony Kenny, *L'essere secondo Tommaso d'Aquino. Un'ontologia problematica*, a cura di Giovanni Ventimiglia, Roma, Carocci, 2014. — Italian translation with critical Italian editorial apparatus, examining twelve senses of "being" in Aquinas.

- Ralph McInerny, *L'analogia in Tommaso d'Aquino*, a cura di F. Di Blasi e L.S. Brock, Roma, Armando, 1999. — Italian translation of the foundational monograph on Thomistic analogy.

**Articles**

- Enrico Berti, "Il 'tomismo analitico' e il dibattito sull'*Esse ipsum*," in *Giornale di Metafisica*, 31 (2009), pp. 5–23. — Examines the analytic-Thomist debate over *Esse ipsum subsistens*, tracing the Aristotelian origins of Kenny's and Geach's objections. Reprinted in E. Berti, *Nuovi studi aristotelici*, iv/2, Morcelliana, 2010, pp. 495–509.

- Emanuela Cosi, "Analogia ed omonimia relativa: Aristotele e San Tommaso," in *Acta Philosophica*, 13/2 (2004), pp. 229–247. — On the relationship between analogy and *pros hen* homonymy from Aristotle to Aquinas, treating the *medietas* between univocity and equivocity and the *ordo ad unum*.

- "Alle origini dell'*analogia entis*" [article on Alexander of Aphrodisias and the genesis of the doctrine], in *Rivista di Filosofia Neo-Scolastica*, 3/2024, pp. 619–636. — Very recent article (2024) tracing Alexander of Aphrodisias's role in the process leading to Aquinas's formulation of the *analogia entis*.

## 6. Kenosi cristologica e linguaggio teologico

The article argues that the Christological kenosis (Phil 2:5-11) provides the ontological foundation for theological language: the Incarnation is the condition of possibility for speech about God, and the formula of Chalcedon functions as a "grammar" of theological discourse. Maximus the Confessor's theology of the *logoi* mediates between divine transcendence and linguistic accessibility.

**Monographs on kenosis and Christology**

- Bruno Forte, *Gesù di Nazaret, storia di Dio, Dio della storia. Saggio di una cristologia come storia*, Cinisello Balsamo (MI), San Paolo, 1981 (rist. fino al 1994). — Foundational Italian christological work developing the dialectic of kenosis-glorification and the connection between theological language and incarnational theology.

- Bruno Forte, *Trinità come storia. Saggio sul Dio cristiano*, Cinisello Balsamo (MI), San Paolo, 1985 (8ª ed. 2010). — Trinitarian kenosis at the core: the Incarnation as simultaneously "kenosi" and "splendore" of Trinitarian love in history. Theological language is rethought from the Trinitarian event.

- Piero Coda, *L'Altro di Dio. Rivelazione e kenosi in S. Bulgakov*, Roma, Città Nuova, 1998. — Monographic study on Bulgakov's sophiology and kenosis; analyzes the relationship between revelation and divine self-emptying, with implications for the possibility of theological language.

- Piero Coda, *Teo-logia. La Parola di Dio nelle parole dell'uomo*, Roma, PUL-Mursia, 1997 (2ª ed. rivista: Lateran University Press, 2004). — On the possibility of theological language: how the Word of God expresses itself in human words. Treats the Christological-Trinitarian foundation of discourse about God.

- Piero Coda, *Dalla Trinità. L'avvento di Dio tra storia e profezia*, Roma, Città Nuova, 2011 (2ª ed. 2012), pp. 600. — Extensive reworking of the Trinitarian tradition including kenosis as a central theological category.

- Emmanuele Rotundo, *Kenosi di «unus de trinitate». Cristo dal Padre nello Spirito*, Assisi, Cittadella, 2017. — "Hypostatic Christology" within a Trinitarian horizon, faithful to the dogma of Chalcedon. Seeks to harmonize the permanence of the two natures with the scriptural affirmation of the Son's self-emptying (Phil 2:7). Directly pertinent to the Chalcedon-kenosis-theological-language nexus.

**Journal special issue**

- *Filosofia e Teologia*, XXXVII/1 (2023): «Kenosis. Autosvuotarsi e incarnarsi del divino» (fascicolo monografico). — Special issue entirely dedicated to kenosis, with essays by M. Lenzi ("Antropologia e teocentrismo nel pensiero cristologico di Tommaso d'Aquino"), L. Messinese ("Incarnazione e kenosi in Simone Weil"), and others. Covers the theme from patristics to Hegel and beyond.

**Maximus the Confessor**

- Massimo il Confessore, *Ambigua: problemi metafisici e teologici su testi di Gregorio di Nazianzo e Dionigi Areopagita*, introduzione, traduzione, note e apparati di Claudio Moreschini, Milano, Bompiani, 2003. — Foundational Italian edition of the *Ambigua*, the key work for Maximus's theology of the *logoi*. Moreschini is one of Italy's foremost patristic scholars.

- Claudio Moreschini, "La persona umana secondo Massimo il Confessore," in *La teologia dal V all'VIII secolo fra sviluppo e crisi. XLI Incontro di Studiosi dell'Antichità Cristiana* (Roma, 2013), Roma, Istituto Patristico Augustinianum, 2014, pp. 697–716. — On personhood in Maximus, essential for the nexus between Chalcedonian Christology and the theology of the *logoi*.

## 7. Orizzonti comparativi: Nāgārjuna e buddhismo Mādhyamaka

The article places Christian apophaticism in comparative dialogue with Nāgārjuna's *śūnyatā*, the two truths (*saṃvṛti-satya / paramārtha-satya*), and the *catuṣkoṭi* (tetralemma).

**Critical editions and translations**

- Nāgārjuna, *Il cammino di mezzo (Madhyamakakarika). Ediz. multilingue*, testo e traduzione di Marcello Meli, a cura e con commento di Emanuela Magno, Padova, Unipress, 2004, pp. 285. — The only complete Italian scholarly edition of the *Mūlamadhyamakakārikā*, with a substantial philosophical commentary by Magno (pp. 153–273).

- Raniero Gnoli (a cura di), *La rivelazione del Buddha*, vol. 2: *Il Grande Veicolo*, con contributi di C. Cicuzza, F. Sferra, M. Maggi, C. Pecchia, Milano, Mondadori ("I Meridiani – Classici dello spirito"), 2004, pp. 1728. — Monumental anthology of Mahāyāna Buddhist texts including Nāgārjuna's *Madhyamakakarika* (pp. 585–656). Section 3, "Il cammino di mezzo," is directly on Madhyamaka.

- Raniero Gnoli (a cura di), *La rivelazione del Buddha*, vol. 1: *I testi antichi*, Milano, Mondadori ("I Meridiani"), 2001, pp. 1461. — Companion volume covering early Buddhism, providing foundational context for the Madhyamaka developments.

- Jan Westerhoff, *Nāgārjuna. Un'introduzione alla filosofia della Via di mezzo*, traduzione di M. Passavanti, Roma, Astrolabio Ubaldini, 2022. — Italian translation of the authoritative philosophical introduction to Nāgārjuna, treating *svabhāva*, *śūnyatā*, epistemology, and the two truths.

**Monographs by Italian scholars**

- Giuseppe Ferraro, *Il buddhismo indiano. Storia di una tradizione filosofica*, Roma, Carocci ("Studi superiori"), 2024, pp. 336. — The most recent and comprehensive Italian treatment of Indian Buddhist philosophy by the leading Italian Madhyamaka specialist. Covers *śūnyatā*, the two truths, and the entire Madhyamaka tradition. Ferraro trained under Raniero Gnoli at La Sapienza.

- Emanuela Magno, *Nagarjuna. Logica, dialettica e soteriologia*, Milano, Mimesis ("Pensieri d'Oriente"), 2012, pp. ~280. — Major Italian monograph on Nāgārjuna's critical method (*prasaṅga*), the relationship between the "middle way" and *vacuità* (*śūnyatā*), and the dismantling of claims to substantial identity.

- Giangiorgio Pasqualotto, *Il buddhismo. I sentieri di una religione millenaria*, Milano, Bruno Mondadori, 2003. — Scholarly introduction covering Madhyamaka and key Buddhist conceptual figures, by Italy's leading philosopher of intercultural comparison (Univ. of Padua).

- Giangiorgio Pasqualotto, *Dieci lezioni sul buddhismo*, Venezia, Marsilio, 2008. — Ten philosophical lectures on core Buddhist themes including emptiness, impermanence, and the Middle Way.

**Articles**

- Emanuela Magno, "Dal pensiero alla vacuità. La critica nāgārjuniana e il trascendentale," in *Il prisma trascendentale. I colori del reale*, I (2014), pp. 153–186. — Placing Nāgārjuna's critique within the framework of transcendental philosophy.

- Giuseppe Ferraro, "A proposito di una recente interpretazione di Pramāṇasamuccaya 5.46," in *Annali dell'Università degli Studi di Napoli "L'Orientale"*, vol. 71, 2011, pp. 205–214. — Italian-language article by the leading Italian Buddhist philosophy specialist.

## 8. Orizzonti comparativi: Śaṅkara e Advaita Vedānta

The article follows Rudolf Otto's *Mysticism East and West* in comparing Eckhart with Śaṅkara on the distinction *nirguṇa/saguṇa Brahman*, the formula *neti neti*, and the concept of *māyā*.

**Foundational comparative work**

- Rudolf Otto, *Mistica orientale, mistica occidentale. Interpretazione e confronto*, traduzione di Marco Vannini, Milano, Edizioni SE ("Conoscenza religiosa"), 2011 (nuova edizione aggiornata, ristampa 2017). — **The** foundational comparative study juxtaposing Eckhart and Śaṅkara. The Italian translation by Vannini — Italy's foremost Eckhart scholar — is the standard reference for the Eckhart–Śaṅkara comparison. Treats *nirguṇa Brahman*, *neti neti*, *māyā*, and parallels with Christian speculative mysticism.

**Italian monographs on comparative mysticism**

- Marco Vannini, *La mistica delle grandi religioni*, Firenze, Le Lettere ("Saggi"), 2010, pp. 396 (2ª ristampa 2016). — Comprehensive comparative study of mysticism across Hinduism, Buddhism, Judaism, Islam, and Christianity. Dedicated chapters on *mistica induista* (including Śaṅkara, Brahman, *ātman*, "tat tvam asi") and *mistica buddhista* (including Nāgārjuna). Directly compares Eastern and Western apophatic traditions.

- Marco Vannini, *Introduzione alla mistica*, Firenze, Le Lettere, 2021. — Includes thematic sections on "Buddhismo e mistica" (§12), "Induismo e mistica" (§13), and comparative themes: *distacco*, *nulla*, the *fond dell'anima*. Vannini explicitly treats Śaṅkara alongside Eckhart on negative theology.

- Marco Vannini, *Oltre il cristianesimo. Da Eckhart a Le Saux*, Milano, Bompiani, 2013 (nuova edizione riveduta: Lindau, 2025). — Traces the mystical-philosophical tradition from Eckhart through modernity, engaging deeply with East-West dialogue.

- Marco Vannini, *Mistica e filosofia*, Firenze, Le Lettere, 2007. — On the convergence between Eckhart's thought and Eastern philosophical traditions (Śaṅkara, Buddhism). Argues for the rational, philosophical, and universal character of mysticism.

**On Śaṅkara and Advaita Vedānta**

- Paul Martin-Dubost, *Samkara e il Vedanta*, trad. it., Roma, Edizioni Asram Vidyā ("Vidyā"). — Italian-language monograph on Śaṅkara and Advaita Vedānta, covering the doctrine of non-duality. Includes translated selections from *Vivekacūḍāmaṇi*, *Upadeśasāhasrī*, *Ātmabodha*.

**Intercultural philosophy**

- Giangiorgio Pasqualotto, *East & West. Identità e dialogo interculturale*, Venezia, Marsilio, 2003. — Methodological foundation for intercultural philosophical comparison between Eastern and Western thought, including Hindu-Christian dialogue.

- Giangiorgio Pasqualotto, "Il sé nell'Induismo e il sé nel Buddhismo," in A. Cislaghi – K. Del Toso (a cura di), *Intrecci Filosofici*, Milano, Mimesis, 2012, pp. 99–106. — Directly compares the concept of self in Hinduism (*ātman-Brahman* identity) and Buddhism (*anātman*).

- Raimon Panikkar, *Religione e religioni*, Brescia, Morcelliana, 1964; also in R. Panikkar – M. Carrara-Pavan (a cura di), *Opera Omnia*, vol. II, Milano, Jaca Book, 2011, pp. 7–168. — Panikkar's foundational work on interreligious dialogue, with deep engagement in Hindu-Christian encounter. Panikkar is the key figure in Italian-language Hindu-Christian comparative theology.

- Francesco Camera – Roberto Celada Ballanti (a cura di), *Filosofia e dialogo tra le religioni*, Brescia, Morcelliana (in «Giornale di Metafisica»). — Collected essays on philosophy and interreligious dialogue.

## 9. Filosofia medievale del linguaggio

The article presupposes the broader medieval tradition of the *proprietates terminorum*, theories of *significatio*, and the constitutive tension between logic and theology.

**Foundational works**

- Alfonso Maierù, *Terminologia logica della tarda scolastica*, Roma, Edizioni dell'Ateneo ("Lessico intellettuale europeo," vol. 8), 1972, pp. 688. — Foundational systematic study of medieval logical terminology (*proprietates terminorum*, *suppositio*, *significatio*, *appellatio*) from the 12th to 15th centuries. Seven chapters tracing the main doctrines of the *logica modernorum* from Aristotle and Boethius through late-antique grammar to the 14th–15th-century systematizations. Remains the essential reference.

- M. Lenzi, C.A. Musatti, L. Valente (a cura di), *Medioevo e filosofia. Per Alfonso Maierù*, Roma, Viella, 2013. — Festschrift for Maierù with multiple essays by leading Italian scholars on medieval logic, semantics, and the *proprietates terminorum* tradition.

**Monographs on medieval semiotics and language**

- Costantino Marmo, *Semiotica e linguaggio nella Scolastica: Parigi, Bologna, Erfurt, 1270–1330. La semiotica dei Modisti*, Roma, Istituto Storico Italiano per il Medio Evo ("Nuovi Studi Storici," 26), 1994, pp. 526. — Major monograph on the Modistae (*grammatica speculativa*, *modi significandi*), covering Parisian, Bolognese, and Erfurt traditions with attention to the interplay between logic, grammar, and theology.

- Costantino Marmo, *La semiotica del XIII secolo tra arti liberali e teologia*, Milano, Bompiani, 2010. — Study of 13th-century semiotics spanning the liberal arts and theology. Examines the revision of sign theory from Augustine through the *logica nova*, the *suppositio* tradition, the *modi significandi*, and pragmatic language use tracing back to Anselm. Bridges Topics 2 and 9.

- Luisa Valente, *Filosofie del medioevo. Essere, felicità, linguaggio* (con la collaborazione di Massimiliano Lenzi), Firenze, Le Monnier Università (Mondadori Education), 2023. — Recent university manual with "linguaggio" as one of its three central themes. Contains chapters on Anselm, Abelard, Gilbert of Poitiers, and the development of 12th-century logic-theology interactions. Based on Valente's prior research on *significatio*, *suppositio*, *translatio*, and the construction of theological discourse.

- Luisa Valente, *«Phantasia contrarietatis». Arti del linguaggio e teologia in Pietro Cantore*, Firenze, SISMEL–Edizioni del Galluzzo, 1997. — On the arts of language (logic, grammar) and theology in Peter the Chanter. Examines the semantic tools developed at Paris for theological discourse.

- Luisa Valente, *Concreti e dividui. Il lessico filosofico di Gilberto di Poitiers*, Roma, Edizioni di Storia e Letteratura, 2022. — On Gilbert of Poitiers's innovative semantic and ontological terminology in his commentaries on Boethius's theological treatises. Relevant for the relationship between logic, language, and Trinitarian theology.

- Alfredo Di Giorgio, *Insolubilia e proprietates terminorum*, Roma, Aracne, 2015. — On the medieval *Insolubilia* literature and the *proprietates terminorum* tradition, with both philological and theoretical approaches.

**Works by Umberto Eco**

- Umberto Eco, *Scritti sul pensiero medievale*, Milano, Bompiani ("Il pensiero occidentale"), 2012, pp. 1332. — Comprehensive collection of Eco's scholarly writings on medieval thought spanning sixty years: medieval semantics, the *arbor porphyriana*, metaphor theory, and sign theory.

- Umberto Eco, *La ricerca della lingua perfetta nella cultura europea*, Roma-Bari, Laterza ("Fare l'Europa"), 1993 (rist. in "Economica Laterza"), pp. 432. — On the dream of a perfect language, with chapters on medieval linguistic theory, Lullian combinatorics, and the relationship between grammar, logic, and theological language.

**Contextual**

- M.T. Bettetini, L. Bianchi, C. Marmo, P. Porro, *Filosofia medievale*, Milano, Raffaello Cortina, 2004, pp. 410. — Anthology with scholarly introductions by four leading Italian medievalists, including Porro. Addresses the intersection of logic, language, and theology.

- R. Fedriga – S. Puggioni (a cura di), *Logica e linguaggio nel Medioevo*, Milano, LED Edizioni Universitarie. — Collected volume on logic and language in the Middle Ages.

## 10. Teologia negativa as a broad tradition

These works provide the overarching frame for the article's argument — the long tradition from Plato through Neoplatonism to medieval Christian theology of attempting (and failing, productively) to say the unsayable.

**Monographs**

- Giovanni Zuanazzi, *Pensare l'assente. Alle origini della teologia negativa*, Roma, Città Nuova ("Idee. Filosofia"), 2005, pp. 200. — Traces the formation of negative theology as a tradition from the Presocratics through early Christian authors, late-antique Neoplatonism, to the early Middle Ages (Eriugena, Eckhart, Cusanus). Explores the impossibility of language comprehending the divine essence. The closest single Italian monograph to the article's core problematic.

- Marco Vannini, *Storia della mistica occidentale. Dall'Iliade a Simone Weil*, Firenze, Le Lettere, 2005 (nuova edizione 2015). — Comprehensive history of Western mysticism from antiquity to the present, treating the continuity of the apophatic tradition through Pseudo-Dionysius, Eriugena, Eckhart, Cusanus.

- Jean-Luc Marion, *Dio senza essere*, trad. it., Milano, Jaca Book ("Filosofia"), 1987; nuova edizione accresciuta 2018, pp. 296. — Fundamental work arguing for overcoming the "idolatric" concept of God created by onto-theo-logy. The 2018 edition includes "San Tommaso e l'onto-teo-logia." Central to discussions of the relationship between negative theology and phenomenology.

**Neoplatonic foundations**

- Werner Beierwaltes, *Pensare l'Uno. Studi sulla filosofia neoplatonica e sulla storia dei suoi influssi*, trad. it. di M. Gatti, Milano, Vita e Pensiero ("Temi metafisici e problemi del pensiero antico," 17), 1992, pp. 400. — On the philosophy of the One in Plotinus, Proclus, Boethius, Eriugena, Cusanus, and the unfolding of unity. Fundamental for understanding the metaphysical framework of negative theology.

- Werner Beierwaltes, *Platonismo nel cristianesimo*, trad. it. di M. Falcioni, intro. di Giovanni Reale, Milano, Vita e Pensiero ("Temi metafisici e problemi del pensiero antico," 78), 2000. — Examines the influence and transformation of Platonism within Christian philosophy from the 4th to the 15th century, covering the key period of Dionysian reception in the Latin West.

- Werner Beierwaltes, *Proclo. I fondamenti della sua metafisica*, trad. it. di N. Scotti, intro. di G. Reale, Milano, Vita e Pensiero ("Metafisica del Platonismo," 5), 1988, pp. 435. — Foundational for understanding Proclean triadic metaphysics, key background to Pseudo-Dionysius's thought.

- Endre von Ivánka, *Platonismo cristiano. Recezione e trasformazione del platonismo nella patristica*, trad. di Enrico Peroli, Milano, Vita e Pensiero, 1992, pp. XLII + 403. — Classic study of how Platonism was received and transformed in patristic thought, central to the Neoplatonism-Christianity nexus.

**General medieval context**

- Giulio d'Onofrio, *Storia del pensiero medievale*, Roma, Città Nuova, 2011, pp. 877. — Major Italian manual of medieval philosophical-theological thought with substantial treatment of all figures discussed in the article.

- Loris Sturlese, *Filosofia nel medioevo*, Roma, Carocci, 2014. — General manual of medieval philosophy by one of Italy's leading medieval philosophy scholars.

**Journal special issue**

- *PATH* (Pontificia Academia Theologica), vol. 20, 2021/2: *Teologia negativa* (fascicolo monografico). — Special issue entirely dedicated to negative theology. Contains articles on the utility and necessity of rediscovering negative theology, negative theology in the Church Fathers, biblical roots of apophatic theology, medieval apophasis (Anselm, Bonaventure, Aquinas), and Riccardo Ferri on "teologia negativa" in Thomas Aquinas across four major works.

## A note on untranslated reference works

Two foundational Anglophone works frequently cited in Italian scholarship on negative theology — Deirdre Carabine, *The Unknown God: Negative Theology in the Platonic Tradition* (Louvain, 1995) and Denys Turner, *The Darkness of God: Negativity in Christian Mysticism* (Cambridge, 1995) — **do not appear to have been translated into Italian**. They are extensively cited by Italian scholars but remain available only in English. Marion's *Dieu sans l'être*, by contrast, is available in Italian as *Dio senza essere* (Jaca Book, 1987/2018), and Otto's *Mystik des Ostens und des Westens* is available as *Mistica orientale, mistica occidentale* (Edizioni SE, 2011), translated by Vannini himself.
